Hello, I'm Maxim!

I recently obtained my PhD in machine learning and I am currently looking for new exciting opportunities in machine learning and deep learning! I am interested in extending AI techniques to provide useful tools to relevant real-world challenges.

In the past years, I worked on developing new methods in generative AI (see e.g. my conference talk on our work in discovering novel molecules), on computer vision applications for object detection in challenging real-world problems (see e.g. my NeurIPS workshop talk on erosion site detection), and on improving our insights on why neural networks work that remarkably well (see e.g. our UAI paper).

I completed my PhD as a doctoral researcher in computer science in the Biomedical Data Analysis group of Professor Volker Roth. Before that I obtained my Bachelor’s and Master’s degree in physics at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology (ETH) in Zurich and spent a semester in the Complex Systems Master’s programme at École normale supérieure (ENS) de Lyon.

Currently, I lecture classes on Python programming and machine learning.
